Medical device supply chains are incredibly complex. When manufacturers, suppliers, and sellers operate in informational silos, a tiny shift in hospital demand can trigger massive, costly fluctuations upstream—a phenomenon known as the **bullwhip effect**.

To tackle this, I built the **MedTech Supply Chain Control Tower**. My goal was to bridge the gap between complex logistical data and executive financial strategy by combining narrative-driven design with a deep, interactive analytics dashboard.

### Project Snapshot: A Two-Pronged Approach

This project blends two complementary experiences to ensure that data is not just accessible, but actionable:

#### 1. The Scrollytelling Narrative

Data without context is just noise. Before throwing users into a complex dashboard, I built a frontend scrollytelling experience. As users scroll, the interface progressively reveals the danger of information silos, explains the physical flow of the supply chain, and builds intuition around how we can use data to solve operational bottlenecks.

#### 2. The Interactive Analytics Engine (R Shiny)

The live dashboard is where the actual analysis happens. It lets users explore metrics, trends, and operational patterns interactively. Highlights include:

* **Capital Exposure Sankey Diagrams:** A dynamic flow chart that lets users toggle between "Unit Volume" and "Capital Mode," instantly revealing exactly how many millions of dollars are tied up in stagnant inventory.
* **Predictive Profitability:** An integrated ARIMA time-series model that forecasts true hospital demand and automatically subtracts existing downstream inventory to recommend a highly optimized production plan.

### What's Next?

Building this Control Tower highlighted the power of unifying physical logistics with financial metrics. I plan to continue iterating on both the narrative and the dashboard—refining the UI, adding deeper interactive drill-downs, and enhancing the decision-support models for end users.
